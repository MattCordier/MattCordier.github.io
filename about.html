"The first duty of love is to listen…"

- Paul Tillich

Making music beings with the simple act of listening.

It's important to not only develop an ear that hears but also a mind that understands.

As a musician, I believe the greatest thing we can share is the ability to listen deeply.

My varied carreer in music has exposed me people and cultures wildly different from my own.

It is a delight to experience the ways in which we, through listening to one another, can quickly create a bond of mutual respect.

I do believe in the power of music to bring unity where there is discord.

With that said, below is a summary of my musical experiences and people I've been fortunate enough to work with…

Matt Cordier is a Milwaukee based musician and educator. Matt has over a decade of teaching experience in private and group settings.

He served four years as a staff member of Yamaha’s Sounds of Summer program. He has also coached the percussion sections of Elmwood Park High School, South Elgin High School, and St. Patrick’s Elementary School.

Matt focuses his private lessons on giving his students the fundamental tools they need to excel as performers.

Matt’s own performance career has included a diverse range of projects. He is the current drummer for The Hot & Dirty Brass Band.

Past highlights include the award winning reggae band Roots Rock Society, trance-reggae band Long Time Friction, cabaret act Kit Kat Honeys, industrial rock legends Christ Analogue, downtempo act The Atomica Project, rock-a-billy sweetheart Suzie G, and an electro-house drumming residency with DJ D-Rex at the Original Mothers.

Matt has accompanied dance classes for Columbia College, The Chicago Academy for the Arts, and The Academy of Movement and Music.

Matt received his early training through the school band system. Attending a Berklee Summer Performance Program in high school solidified his commitment to be a professional musician.

Matt graduated from Northern Illinois University with a degree in music and dance performance. He continued his music education through intensive study with private teachers.

Matt has been fortunate enough to take lessons and master classes with some drumming giants including Kenny Aronoff, Johnny Rabb, Paul Wertico, Carl Allen, Zoro, Glenn Kotche, Jim Riley, Dave Dicenso, and Jason McGerr.
